What is Bonus Arbitrage?

Bonus arbitrage is straightforward: companies offer cash rewards for new signups that exceed the cost of trying their product. Sometimes they pay you just for signing up, hoping you'll become a long-term customer.

The Core Strategy

  • Find high-value signup bonuses — Banks and fintech companies offer $50-$500 for opening accounts
  • Complete minimal requirements — Direct deposit setup, small deposits, or 30-day account holds
  • Collect your bonus — Get paid through tracking platforms that handle everything
  • Move on or cancel — Most offers allow account closure after bonuses without penalty

The key insight: these aren't "deals"—they're pure profit opportunities. When a bank pays you $480 to redirect one paycheck, that's free money for 15 minutes of work. When a brokerage pays you $30 for depositing $5, that's a 500% return in days.

Why This Works

Customer acquisition costs are astronomical. Banks and fintech companies spend $200-$500 per customer through traditional marketing. It's cheaper to pay you directly.

They're betting on retention. Most people forget to cancel or generate revenue through fees and interest. But if you're disciplined, you take the bonus and leave.

Competition drives offers higher. As new companies launch, signup bonuses increase. This creates perfect arbitrage opportunities.
